Category Titles

All workshops are seperated into different categories in the build menu. These category titles can also be build, they will spell out their name. This can be used to label areas ingame, which is great for succession forts. They cost no buildmaterials.

WARLOCK-SPECIFIC: Sapient creatures (invaders) leave a scalp when being butchered. They rot after 2 weeks, but in that time the tanner can make a skin-cover from them. These are used for books and armor-decorations.

TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell and scales into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system.

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: Tan stomachs directly into a waterskins, rope from guts/intestines and pelts into a furs. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

Every megabeast will leave a special corpse in this mod and can be butchered in the slaughterhouse to receive 3 items. A trophy that can be kept in a display case or traded away, a special reward that is specific to the creature, and a megabeast soul. The soul can be used to revive the beast in the Necromantic Altar. Keep in mind that titans and forgotten beasts are not megabeasts and cant be affected by modding.

Megabeasts also give 60 meat, or 50 boulders, or 25 metal bars upon being butchered, depending on the bodytype of the beast. You will always get 1 heart.

Example: Kill dragon, butcher dragon corpse, get 10 straps of dragonscale. A steel quality, fireproof leather type. You will also get the dragon skull, worth a lot, and the soul.

Scour the graveyard for items, old corpses, bones and lost souls. Maybe you even find a ghoul scavenging across the tombs, ready to be recruited into your dungeon.

Scouring for leftovers has a 3% chance of netting you a coffin, a totem or a slab.

Scouring for corpses has a 3% chance of spawning an "old corpse", which can be butchered for bones and a skull. You must view the "old corpse" and declare if for butchering manually.

Scouring for souls has a 3% chance of spawning a soul. It will wither away after a month, just like any other soul.

Attracting a zombie costs a stack of meat, and has a 25% chance of spawning a opposed-to-life zombie, which will randomly stumble around the map. They cause miasma underground, so make sure to do this on the surface. They might hunt wildlife and attack invaders, but you cant control them.

Attracting a ghoul costs a stack of meat and a stack of bones, and has a 5% chance of spawning a new ghoul civ-member. It also has a 2% chance of spawning a feral ghoul, who will attack everything on sight. You have been warned.

Throne Room -

Build labor

building_throneroom.txt

The Throne Room serves one purpose, and one purpose only: To appoint your Overlord. Any Warlock can be elevated to this position.

Actually thats a lie. You can also commandeer your minions here. Send them against churches, villages, towns, caravans and castles. If you dont have the necessary intel to do so, start with sending them out at random. To get intel torture and interrogate prisoners in your Torture Chamber or Interrogation Room.

How it works:

You use Workshop Profiles to link the Overlord to this workshop. Only he can gather his minions and give commands. Make some raiding kits in the Corrupted Forge. They only cost a bag (for snatching) and a ironbone bar (for a basic weapon).

You select a command.

Steal will bring more loot. - Low chance of counter-attacks.

Kidnap will bring more prisoners. - Medium chance of counter-attacks.

Kill will bring more scalps. - High chance of counter-attacks.

You select a target.

Countryside - Needs 1 raiding kit - Low chance of success. No counter-attacks.

Caravan - Needs 1 raiding kit and info - Certainty of getting goods, but medium chance for loot, and low chance for prisoners or scalps. Low chance of Counter-attacks possible.

Church - Needs 2 raiding kits and info - Certainty of prisoners, loot or scalps. Takes a bit longer. Counter-attacks possible.

Village - Needs 3 raiding kits and info - Certainty of several prisoners, loot or scalps. Takes longer. Counter-attacks possible.

Town - Needs 4 raiding kits and info - Certainty of a lot of prisoners, loot or scalps. Takes longer. Counter-attacks possible.

Castle - Needs 5 raiding kits, 3 ballista parts, 3 catapult parts and info - Certainty of all rewards at the same time, with extra amounts of the one chosen. No counter-attacks.

Once you picked a target and a command, all other reactions are chained automatically. You will get announcements in your gamelog that let you know whats happened at the moment.

After the raid, four reactions show your results: Goods (items), Loot (treasures), Scalps (scalps for books) and Prisoners (live prisoners). They are unpacked at the throne.

Torture a Warlock: The worker has to be a Warlock. He will be injured and slowed down, but all other Warlocks in your dungeon will take it as a warning and work faster for one month.

Corrupt caged Invader: Bring any cage with a captured invader nearby. It has to be a sapient being, no animals or warbeasts. You can then corrupt your prisoner into a ghoul, which will join your dungeon. Frost Giants and Automatons can not be corrupted.

Torture a Prisoner: Pasture a prisoner on the workshop first. Once you do the reaction, the prisoner will be injured. He will drop a piece of intel, which can be checked in the Interrogation Room. It might help you raid specific targets. You can only torture prisoners that are concious, so dont waste your time on heavily injured ones. You can not butcher the corpse, if the prisoner dies from torture.

9 different sections are available, sorted by direction. You can build just one, or assemble them as you see fit. Each section needs 10 books to be build, these can be any books you have. Steal them, buy them, write your own, anything goes. The library sections do train magical spells to Warlocks and unlock new workshop.

One tip: Dont try to memorize the costs for spells. Just strip mine and shredder everything.

Lets start with the center.

Center Library: Scriptorium

The only section that does not train spells. Here you can make bind books, produce ink and make transcripts. Its the basis for your library, because books are the build-mats for all other library sections.

You require a scalp, ink, paper and glue to make a book. You can make ink from blood, ash or dye in the center library, filling three jugs with each reaction. You can boil glue from hoof, horns or bone in the Kitchen. You can press wood into paper in the Screw Press.

Books are bound in skins of sapient creatures, like dwarves or humans. Every invader that is butchered gives you one scalp, which is made into a skin-cover in the Tanners Shop. 10 books are needed per library.

A special book can be written as well: The Tome of Sator. It requires a scalp, glue, ink and paper, just like a normal book, but has 50 worksteps, each one adding a page. This means it takes a long time and costs 50 units of vellum or paper to write it. It unlocks the workshop: The Square of Sator.

Top-Left (North-West): Fire.

A special book can be written: The Book of Eibon. It costs the same as any normal book, with the additional cost of 5 skin-covers, gold, silver and iron dust. It is a component to unlock the high-ritual workshop: The Grand Convocation.

Write the Codex of the Abyss. Unlocks The Abyss. Costs 5 skin-cover, 2 glue, 10 pages, 1 ink.

Learn Burn, Fire Jet, Fire Ball, Incinerate, Dragonfire.

- Burn is a close-combat spell that adds pain and blisters, but starts no fires. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems and 1 circle of protection to learn.

- Fire Jet allows a short-range breath of fire. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems, 2 eerie flames and 1 circle of protection to learn.

- Fire Ball is a projectile weapon that can start fires. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems, 2 eerie flames and 2 circles of protection.

- Incinerate is a projectile weapon with AoE fire effect on impact. Very hot and friendly fire is possible. It costs 4 candles, 4 eerie flames, 4 greater circles of protection, 1 bag of aluminum powder and 1 large gem.

- Dragonfire allows a short-range breath of dragonfire. Very hot and friendly fire is possible. It costs 4 candles, 4 eerie flames, 1 greater circles of protection, 1 bag of aluminum powder and 1 large gem.

- Raise Dead allows to animate all corpses near the Warlock. It costs 1 circle of protection and 1 corpse.

The Abyss allows summoning demons from the underworld.

You have two different types of reactions: The summon any demon, which requires slade, or summoning a specific demon type.

Summon any demon: Costs 4 candles, 1 greater circle of protection, 1 bag of slade dust and 1 soul: This will spawn one of 20 possible procedually generated demons. Exactly the same ones you might meet in hell. They are either friendly, civ members or pets. Most of them you will not be able to control, but they will hang around your meeting areas and fight for you. These demons are different in every world you play.

Summon a specific demon: Costs 4 candles, 1 greater circle of protections and a number of souls indicated in the reaction name. The better the creature you want, the more expensive the summoning. You can summon following creatures:

-Sinister Hierophants (fallen human doctors) - costs 2 Souls

- Harpies (flying humanoids with claws) - costs 3 Souls

- Holistic Spawns (humanoids with a mouth in their chest) - costs 5 Souls

- Hulking Black Barghests (big evil dogs) - costs 7 Souls

- Deep One (large mutated fish-humanoid) - costs 10 Souls

- Shadow Weaver (humanoid demon that spews webs) - costs 12 Souls

- Icy Blue Ghast (flying ice demon) - costs 15 Souls

- Manticore (half lion, half scoprion with powerful venom) - costs 20 Souls

- Black Marble Gargoyle (flying rock monster) - costs 25 Souls

- Plague Wraith (megabeast with natural scythes) - costs 35 Souls

- Balrog (burning megabeast with AoE effect) - costs 50 Souls

Make an infernal trade with the devil. You have to sell a soul and you are asked for a wish. You can type the item name you want, followed by the material you want.

For example: pitchfork - enter - wood - enter. You will receive a wooden pitchfork. The item can be anything, plant, weapon, armor, tool, and the material can be anything as well. Magma pot? Water Breastplate? Ogre Tooth Greataxe? Anything goes.

You have a 65% chance of success, and a 35% chance for something horrible to happen. The worker might die. A hostile bloodbeast might appear. All plants and trees might turn to ashes. A negative thoughts might be doubled in impact. The surface and caverns might go up in flames.

But than again, the masterwork adamantine long sword is calling..... just one soul away.

The greatest magical rituals that you have access to.

Create the Sorcerers Banner: Costs 10 dreadnought bars, 1 totem and 10 books: Warlock body is consumed to create the Banner. Gives all Warlocks +50% Toughness, +50% Agility and 3 additional slade-boulder ranged attacks.

Create the Ghoul Banner: Costs 10 dreadnought bars, 1 totem and 10 corpses: Warlock body is consumed to create the Banner. Gives all Ghouls +50% Strength and the Charge and Berserk ability in combat. //Charge adds short speed boosts, and berserk adds short, but massive attribute boosts.

Create the Skeleton Banner: Costs 10 dreadnought bars, 10 bloodsteel bars and 10 ironbone bars and 1 totem: Warlock body is consumed to create the Banner. Gives all Skeletons +50% Speed and controls them on the entire map. //no need for pylons to control them.

Create the Blood Chalice: Costs 10 dreadnought bars, 1 totem and 10 barrels of blood: Warlock body is consumed to create the Chalice. Removes Bloodsucker from all Vampires. //no need for bloodwine to control them.

Creature the Over-Pylon: Costs 10 dreadnought bars, 1 totem and 10 large gems: Warlock body is consumed to create the Over-Pylon. Gives all Pylons the ability to shoot shards of crystals at enemies, as well as release AoE attacks in close combat.

The Eternal Rose acts like a stasis chamber. You can produce them at the North-Western Library. Any Warlock that is pricked by its thorns will fall asleep on the workshop. They wont move, wont need food nor drink and wont wake up.

Any worker can come by and burn another eternal rose and wake all sleepers with its scent.

This means that you can wall in a handful of Warlocks and a Skeleton worker to wake them up, completely separated from the rest of the fort. While everyone else is dying from diseases, killed by dwarves or demons alike, your time capsule squad will survive and rebuild.

You can also remove all bad thoughts from all citizens, by burning one Eternal Rose in the workshop.

The Enchanter creates animated weapons and armors, as well as enchants items with special effects.

You can take 1 hooked sword, a breastplate, a buckler, a mask, greaves, 2 gauntlets and 2 boots to make a animated suit of armor. This also costs a soul, and generates a very strong fighter.

Animating weapons is easier: Take 1 soul and a fitting weapon, and you get the live version. You can chose between ironbone, bloodsteel or dreadnought materials, and pitchforks, scythes, ritual knives, scourges, serrated axes and bonesaws.
