Category Titles

All workshops are seperated into different categories in the build menu. These category titles can also be build, they will spell out their name. This can be used to label areas ingame, which is great for succession forts. They cost no buildmaterials.

A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell and scales into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system.

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: Tan stomachs directly into a waterskins, rope from guts/intestines and pelts into a furs. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

Every megabeast will leave a special corpse in this mod and can be butchered in the slaughterhouse to receive 3 items. A trophy that can be kept in a display case or traded away, a special reward that is specific to the creature, and a megabeast soul. The soul can be used in the Megabeast Kennels to train a single dwarf instantly to legendary in a chosen combat skill, or to revive the beast. Keep in mind that titans and forgotten beasts are not megabeasts and cant be affected by modding.

Megabeasts also give 60 meat, or 50 boulders, or 25 metal bars upon being butchered, depending on the bodytype of the beast. You will always get 1 heart.

Example: Kill dragon, butcher dragon corpse, get 10 straps of dragonscale. A steel quality, fireproof leather type. You will also get the dragon skull, worth a lot, and the soul.

The Pottery shapes and dries clay items. You can make clay, fire clay and kaolinite items from the collected raw materials, resulting in greenware items. Greenware is a special name for wet clay. The workshop will automatically queue two drying reactions, from greenware to leather-hard, from leather-hard to bone-dry. The items can not be used until they are fired in the Clay Oven.

To make it simpler: Shape Item => Dry => Dry => Fire Item.

The material progression is: Clay => Greenware => Leather-Hard => Bone-Dry => Ceramic

Each reaction has a 5% chance to fail, simulating impurities in the clay.

The ethereal forge can summon weapons and armors from the ether. These items are volatile and will disappear after a short while. You can summon axes, swords, spears, scythes, picks, bows, arrows and quivers, armors and siege ammo here.

Scour the graveyard for items, old corpses, bones and lost souls. Maybe you even find a ghoul scavenging across the tombs, ready to be recruited into your dungeon.

Scouring for leftovers has a 3% chance of netting you a coffin, a totem or a slab.

Scouring for corpses has a 3% chance of spawning an "old corpse", which can be butchered for bones and a skull. You must view the "old corpse" and declare if for butchering manually.

Scouring for souls has a 3% chance of spawning a soul. It will wither away after a month, just like any other soul.

Attracting a zombie costs a stack of meat, and has a 25% chance of spawning a opposed-to-life zombie, which will randomly stumble around the map. They cause miasma underground, so make sure to do this on the surface. They hunt wildlife and attack invaders, but you cant control them.

Attracting a ghoul costs a stack of meat and a stack of bones, and has a 5% chance of spawning a new ghoul civ-member. It also has a 2% chance of spawning a feral ghoul, who will attack everything on sight. You have been warned.

Library Sections -

building_library.txt

9 different sections are available, sorted by direction. You can build just one, or assemble them as you see fit. Each section needs 10 books to be build, these can be any books you have. Steal them, buy them, write your own, anything goes. The library sections do train magical spells to Warlocks and unlock new workshop.

One tip: Dont try to memorize the costs for spells. Just strip mine and shredder everything.

Lets start with the center.

Center Library: Scriptorium

The only section that does not train spells. Here you can make bind books, produce ink and make transcripts. Its the basis for your library, because books are the build-mats for all other library sections.

You require ink, paper and glue to make a book. You can make ink from blood, ash or dye in the center library, filling three jugs with each reaction. You can boil glue from hoof, horns or bone in the Kitchen. You can press wood into paper in the Screw Press.

Books are bound in either leather or bone. 10 books are needed per library.

A special book can be written as well: The Tome of Sator. I required glue, ink and paper, just like a normal book, but has 50 worksteps, each one adding a page. This means it takes a long time and costs 50 units of vellum or paper to write it. It unlocks the workshop: The Square of Sator.

Top-Left (North-West): Fire.

A special book can be written: The Book of Eibon. It costs the same as any normal book, with the additional cost of gold, silver and iron dust. It is a component to unlock the high-ritual workshop: The Grand Convocation.

Write the Codex of the Abyss. Unlocks The Abyss. Costs 1 leather, 2 glue, 10 pages, 1 ink.

Learn Burn, Fire Jet, Fire Ball, Incinerate, Dragonfire.

- Burn is a close-combat spell that adds pain and blisters, but starts no fires. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems and 1 circle of protection to learn.

- Fire Jet allows a short-range breath of fire. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems, 2 eerie flames and 1 circle of protection to learn.

- Fire Ball is a projectile weapon that can start fires. It costs 4 candles, 2 gems, 2 eerie flames and 2 circles of protection.

- Incinerate is a projectile weapon with AoE fire effect on impact. Very hot and friendly fire is possible. It costs 4 candles, 4 eerie flames, 4 greater circles of protection, 1 bag of aluminum powder and 1 large gem.

- Dragonfire allows a short-range breath of dragonfire. Very hot and friendly fire is possible. It costs 4 candles, 4 eerie flames, 1 greater circles of protection, 1 bag of aluminum powder and 1 large gem.

Top (North): Death & Necromancy

A special book can be written: The Necronomicon. It costs the same as any normal book, with the additional cost of 3 stacks of bones and 3 totems. It is a component to unlock the high-ritual workshop: The Grand Convocation.

Wrote the Codex of Flesh and Bones. Unlocks The Fleshsmith. Costs 1 leather, 2 glue, 10 pages, 1 ink.

Learn Curse, Enfeeble, Necrosis, Drain Life, Agony, Shackle, Bone Armor, Raise Dead.

- Curse sets luck to 0 for one enemy. It costs 3 corpses, 1 circle of protection, 1 stack of bones and 1 bag of foul herbs.

- Enfeeble lowers strength. It costs 1 circle of protection, 1 corpse, 3 totems and 1 bag of foul herbs.

- Necrosis rots enemy tissues. It costs 1 circle of protection, 3 corpses, 3 bags of foul herbs, 1 totem and 1 stack of bones.

- Drain Life gives pain and dizzyness to the enemy, and raises recuperation of the Warlock. It costs 1 circle of protection and 4 bags of foul herbs.

- Agony adds pain to the enemy. It costs 1 circle of protection, 3 stacks of bone and 3 totems.

- Shackle impairs enemy limbs. It costs 1 circle of protection, 5 stacks of bone and 5 totems.
