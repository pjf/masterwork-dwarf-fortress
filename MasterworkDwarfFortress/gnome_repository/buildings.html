A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell and scales into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system.

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: Tan stomachs directly into a waterskins, rope from guts/intestines and pelts into a furs. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

The vanilla kiln has been splitted into the kiln and the clay oven. The clay oven can make any items from clay, fireclay or kaolinite, as well as a unit of fuel. Clay produces stoneware items, Fireclay makes earthenware items and kaolinite produces porcelain items.

The "collect clay" zones have been renamed to "collect clay/dirt/peat", since more types of soil are valid targets now. Dirt can only be used as free boulders for constructions, while peat can be compressed and burned as low-cost fuel.

Build labor

Furnace Operating

None
Building is Hardcoded
reaction_smelter.txt

You can use plywood for one unit of charcoal per log, burn compact peat to 2-4 bars of coke and the very valuable anthracite for 12 bars of coke. Plywood is a byproduct of the wood-industry, compact peat can either be found in soil-layers or produced by compressing peat, and anthrazite is a rare coal cluster found deep underground.

You can create a metal anvil from five bars. You no longer need to bring one at embark.

Smelting ore also creates a waste product called Slag. These bars clutter up your smelter and should be destroyed in the slagpit or used up as buildmats.

The Nature Shrine is your connection with the wildlife around you. Your success will be dependant on a number of factors, most importantly the skill of the trainer and the value of the animal, but the natural tendencies of the animal, the general training level, and many other aspects also go into the formula. Gnomes can produce some high-tech tools from very small amounts of metal through an elaborate, multi-step crafting system. The Tinker's Workshop is the spot for creating those small, intricate devices needed for higher-level, heavy-duty equipment, ranging from simple cogs and coils to high-tech devices like clockwork power cells and cathode ray tubes. Most of the tools you make here begin as metal strips, which you can produce by splitting a metal sheet from the forge. Some will also require glass items, usually glass tubes. Actually turning the items into usable devices is done at the Gadgeteer's Workshop.

Drilling Rig -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat
Dimension
 Requirements
Machine Operator
Machine Operating
Drillbit, 4 Blocks, 2 Mechanisms, (z) Pipe Sections, (z) Corkscrews
3x3x(z)
Carpenter/Mason, Mechanic, Metalsmith

One of the finest pieces of gnomish technology, the useful drilling rig is a faster, simpler means of bringing liquids up from deep underground. By adding pipe sections and corkscrews to the original structure, it will make a hole extending down into the depths. It can then draw up liquids. To draw up liquids, the drill requires either mechanical or manual power - machinery can be connected to any of the gears in the corners of the building. You will need a minimum of 10 power for each z-level you want to draw up liquid, although you can add to this by manually pumping. The drill will draw liquids up from one z-level BELOW the tip. It requires a depth of at least 4 to do so. The liquids will emerge one z-level BELOW the building itself. Make sure there is an opening for them to come out of, or they will quickly overflow into the drill's base, leading to soaked floors or (in the case of magma) a roasted pump operator.

Automatic Factories -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat
Dimension
 Requirements
Machine Operator
Machine Operating
4 Blocks, 6-8 Mechanisms, 4 Pipe Sections
3x3
Carpenter/Mason, Mechanic

For efficient production, gnomes rely heavily on their powered, automatic workshops. There are several different kinds, all of which perform different tasks, however they are all set up in more or less the same way. To operate an automatic workshop, you will need to construct both the main body of the workshop, and at least one machine input and one machine output tile adjacent to the main body. When provided with a power source attached to one of the corner tiles, the machine will pull in any items resting on the input tiles, process them, and then deposit them on the far side of the output tiles. By placing an input tile connected to another factory machine in this spot, it is possible to chain factories together and create a fully-automated, customized production facility.

There is a lot to learn about automated factories, their specific functions and their peculiarities. For more information, check the Automachina page.

3x3
Electrical Technology, Gems and Warpstone

The Cosmic Gear runs electrical power through a warpstone calibrated with gem-based flux capacitors, and plugs into the very clockwork of the universe itself. When connected to a power source from above or below, this inexplicable device will accelerate the flow of time, or if reversed, will slow it down to a stop. If you need a caravan to come quickly, or need some extra time to prepare for an invasion, it can be very useful. But beware of meddling too much with the cosmic clock: accelerating time will age your gnomes and animals faster, which can cause unexpected die-offs by old age, and slowing time down seems to have strange effects on your gnomes' abillity to take care of their basic needs. This device has not been adequately tested in all circumstances. Use it at your own risk.

Logic Gates -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat
Dimension
 Requirements
Mechanic
Mechanics
1 Mechanism
3x3
Mechanics

Gnomes can produce a series of special buildings that work as logical operators, allowing fast, efficient computing using axles and gear assemblies, as well as allowing direct interfacing between mechanical parts and gates, without the need for liquid or creature based computing. Each logical operator can take input from any connected axle or power source, with the exception of gear assemblies. The operator will then put the input values together and affect nearby gear assemblies and gates in a predetermined way.


There are 7 types of logical operators:

NOT operators will return TRUE if they are connected to an unpowered input, and FALSE if they are connected to a powered input.

AND operators will return TRUE if they are connected to at least one powered inputs without any unpowered inputs, and FALSE otherwise.

OR operators will return TRUE as long as they are connected to at least one powered input, and FALSE otherwise.

NAND will return TRUE as long as they are connected to at least one unpowered input, and FALSE otherwise

NOR will return TRUE if they are connected to at least one unpowered input without any powered inputs, and FALSE otherwise.

XOR requires both a powered input and an unpowered input to evaluate to TRUE, and will return FALSE otherwise.

XNOR requires either all of its inputs to be powered or all of its inputs to be unpowered to evealuate to TRUE, and will return FALSE otherwise.


After evaluating its inputs, the operator will affect the following devices:


Gear assembly - TRUE: Engaged. FALSE: Disengaged.

Doors, grates, hatches, bars, and floodgates - TRUE: Open. FALSE: Closed.

Upright spikes - TRUE: Retracted. FALSE: Extended.

Bridge - TRUE: Retracted. FALSE: Normal.
