A few words about weapon usage:

Gnomes are excellent artificers. Many of their items have special properties. For the most part, they don't tend to be great fighters at close range, but they have a lot of ranged options, from the crossbows and mechanized weapon shooters they share with the dwarves, to firearms, grenade launchers, and even magnetic railguns. They can also produce some special weapons that don't require ammo to use.

How to make a Railgun (because we know you want to)

You will need:
  • 2 bags of sand
  • 1 piece of cloth
  • 15 bars of metal (any kind)
  • 1 bar of weapon-grade metal (or anything that a crossbow can be made from)
  • 4 bars of magnetic metal (iron, nickel, or cobalt)
  • Iron bars for the ammo (1 bar -> 3 ammo)
  • Access to mechanical power
  • A whole bunch of mechanisms
  • A lot of fuel
  • Make a lot of mechanisms. I'm not even mentioning the mechanisms here, you'll need a lot of them for everything gnomish.
  • Collect sand. If you have no sand, smelt some metal and grind the slag into sand at the Slag Pit.
  • Make a Glass Furnace. Make a glass disk and a glass tube.
  • Make a Metalsmith's Forge. Make 2 metal foil, 2 metal pipe sections, and a metal disk.
  • Get some cloth. Combine the cloth, glass disk, and metal pipe sections into an electrostatic generator. Connect it to a power source.
  • Make a Tinker's Workshop. Combine the glass tube and the 2 metal foil into a capacitor.
  • Charge the capacitor at the electrostatic generator. (The capacitor can be reused.)
  • Get either nickel, cobalt, or iron (preferably not iron, since you'll need that for the ammo). At the smelter, use the capacitor to create a magnet. Do this twice.
  • Combine the metal disk and 2 magnets into a dynamo at the Tinker's Workshop. You now have a dynamo, which has many uses. But if you want a railgun...
  • Make 4 wires and a gun barrel (both under 'other objects') at the Metalsmith's Forge. In place of the gun barrel, you can make a crossbow instead for a magnetic crossbow, which is just as powerful and deals piercing damage.
  • Make 2 more magnets.
  • Combine the magnets, wires, gun barrel (or crossbow), and dynamo into a railgun at the Artificer's Forge. You now have your railgun!
  • What, you want to fire it too? Then make more magnets. You'll need iron magnets, specifically.
  • Convert the iron magnets into magnetized slugs (or bolts) at the Artificer's Forge. Congratulations! You now are in posession of the most powerful kinetic weapon in the gnomes' arsenal. Be warned that it is slow-firing and the ammo is expensive to produce, so don't let it go to waste. Give it to your best riflegnome and give them plenty of support.
