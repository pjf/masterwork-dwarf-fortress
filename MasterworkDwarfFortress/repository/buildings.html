Category Titles

All workshops are seperated into different categories in the build menu. These category titles can also be build, they will spell out their name. This can be used to label areas ingame, which is great for succession forts. They cost no buildmaterials.

Craftsdwarf -

reaction_craftsdwarf.txt

A big amount of new reactions can be done here. You can extract new kinds of metal strands from metals bars, enabling silver, golden, mithril or orichalcum clothing. You can craft single instruments or crafts from boulders, random instruments or toys from boulders, make rope from leather, make candles from tallow or wax. Illumination, like braziers, candelabras and fireplaces can be created from rock boulders.

The crafts section for bone and shell contain all armor items you can usually produce, and you can seperate bone-stacks into single bones. The wooden and rock crafts now include hilts, which are needed for weapon production, as well as smoking pipes. Wooden wagons are used in the expedition system to send your own caravans.

Dye, Blood, Graphite and Ash can be made into ink. You require 3 pots to fill when doing this. Quills can be produced from feathers. Ink and quills are used in the Scriptorium and Printing Press.

With enough feathers and a bed, you can make a feathered matress on a bed, a prized tradegood and high-value item for your dwarves.

Use wicker or hay and a shirt to make Scarecrows. Scarecrows cant fight or move, but are good to reveal ambushes. Pasture them around your fort for early detection and to scare away curious animals.

Many new sorts of food are available. Roasting inedible seeds make them edible, boiling eggs does the same to raw eggs. Mined "living rock" can be butchered for meat, mined "cave fungus" can be made into cheese. Another new cheese can be made from boozebelly goats, which is alcoholic and can be fermented into drinks.

You can use meat to make ground meat or, combined with vermin remains, sausages. You can also extract blood from fresh meat, for all kinds of purposes from sacrifices to dark magic.

You can use flour or sugar milled from plants to either bake bread or make candy. Note that DF cant find ingredients inside bags that are inside barrels. So store this in a stockpile without barrels.

If you farm silk moths you can boil their cocoons here to extract the silk threads.

You can boil horn/hoof materials here to create glue. Glue is needed for binding books and the making of plywood, and other sources include spore-trees or bloated tubers.

Press glue from bloated tubers, used for bookbinding or plywood-making. Other sources are horn/hoof in the Kitchen, or spore-tree resin in the Herbalist's Lab.

Press water from quarry bush leaves into buckets, oil from pig tails, shadowleaf bushes, quarry bushes or hide root into jugs. Press wooden logs into 2 pages of paper. And the most awesome show of dwarven might: Press coal together so hard, that it becomes a diamond. Humans need high-pressure chambers for this, but dwarves do it with their biceps.

The oil can be used to polish wood, harden leather, make soap or burning ammo. The paper you will need for bookbinding, alternatives for it are papyrus (thatchery) or vellum (tanner). Water is, as always, used for injured dwarves.

Tanner -

Build labor
Skill
Buildmat

 Requirements
Tanner

reaction_tanner.txt

TANNING: Adds specific tanning reactions for each animal skin type. You can tan skin, tough skin, chitin, shell, scales or rigid scales, into an amount of leather based on the creatures size. Usually around 2-4 pieces, smaller creatures might give none, while massive monsters give dozens.

LEATHER: Normal leather can be used for suede(3) or vellum(3) from one piece. Suede is very thin leather, useless for combat, but good for backpacks or quivers. Vellum is used for the library system. Alternatives are papyrus (thatchery) or paper (screw press).

FUR/STOMACH/GUT/HORN: The newest additions are the tanning of a stomach directly into a waterskin, the making of rope from guts/intestines and tanning a pelt into a fur. A fur is the very same as leather, but also protects from cold. Fur is needed for glacier embarks. If you dont want the fur, you can shave of the hairs to create a piece of leather and a unit of hair thread. Horns can be shaped into a small dagger, for eariest militias.

FEATHERS: Birds leave feathers upon being butchered, which can be used to make quills for writing, or feather matresses for beds.

UPGRADES: You can upgrade leather by boiling it in oil to harden it, then add bonestuds to the hardened leather to make studded leather, and then stitch several studded leather pieces together to create lamellar leather. Each type will be a little tougher, with lamellar being the best. The oil is preserved to 85%, and studs can be made from bone, vermin or horn/hoof. The same upgrade system in a smaller form applies to shell, scale and chitin. Using 2 units of it you can combine it to one unit of the "rigid" version.

Material strength:

Leather = Vanilla Leather. ::: Tough leather = Copper.

Studded leather = Bronze. ::: Lamellar leather = Iron.

Chitinplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Chitin = Iron, very light.

Scaleplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Scale = Iron, fireproof.

Shellplate = Bronze. ::: Rigid Shellplate = Between iron and steel, heavy.

Only needing a block to be build, this workshop should be constructed early. It automatically queues reactions for smoothing rough wood logs and cutting gnarled roots. You can create bows, upgrade all ranged weapons and make specialized ammo from wood or bone. The Fletchers Workshop is the advancement of the bowyer.

Bows require wood and thread to be made. You have two kinds of upgrades available for ranged weapons, you can either bayonett them using a large dagger, or add 2 mechanisms to them to increase their rate of fire. You can even do both if you wish, creating a bladed repeating weapon.

The ammo comes in three types, broadhead (cutting damage), hammerhead (blunt damage) and piercing (piercing damage). In essence they work the same as sword, hammer and spear. You can make bolts, arrows and javelins from wood and bone. You can find the metal counterparts of this ammo in the Ammo Caster among the furnaces.

The Pottery shapes and dries clay items. You can make clay, fire clay and kaolinite items from the collected raw materials, resulting in greenware items. Greenware is a special name for wet clay. The workshop will automatically queue two drying reactions, from greenware to leather-hard, from leather-hard to bone-dry. The items can not be used until they are fired in the Clay Oven.

To make it simpler: Shape Item => Dry => Dry => Fire Item.

The material progression is: Clay => Greenware => Leather-Hard => Bone-Dry => Ceramic

Each reaction has a 5% chance to fail, simulating impurities in the clay.

Sawmill -

Build labor

The Sawmill is the upgrade to the Wood Splitting Block. It has the same reactions, cutting rough wood and roots, making planks and cleaning sawdust. In addition to that you can make plywood from sawdust and glue.

The most important feature is treefarming. Each kind of tree in the mod can be planted in farmplots, if you are in the correct biome and have the acorns or spores. Trees, Ironbark, Steeloak, Fungiwood, Sporetrees, Crystal Wood, Glassthorns, Nethercaps, Shadethorn, Feather and Glumprong can be farmed. Seeds must be brought at embark, bought from caravans, or discovered from cutting collected tree saplings. The Herablist's Lab might also provide access to seeds.

All trees grow all year and need at least one year to mature. The tree saplings are treated as plants and stored as such. Only the Sawmill can cut them into wood logs.

The Sawmill can also break down any wooden item you made into wooden logs and sawdust.

building_slaughterhouse.txt

Every megabeast will leave a special corpse in this mod and can be butchered in the slaughterhouse to receive 3 items. A trophy that can be kept in a display case or traded away, a special reward that is specific to the creature, and a megabeast soul. The soul can be used in the Megabeast Kennels to train a single dwarf instantly to legendary in a chosen combat skill, or to revive the beast. Keep in mind that titans and forgotten beasts are not megabeasts and cant be affected by modding.

Megabeasts also give 60 meat, or 50 boulders, or 25 metal bars upon being butchered, depending on the bodytype of the beast. You will always get 1 heart.

Example: Kill dragon, butcher dragon corpse, get 10 straps of dragonscale. A steel quality, fireproof leather type. You will also get the dragon skull, worth a lot, and the soul.

Example Rewards:

Colossus: 25 metal bars

Dragon: 10 dragonscales, count as leather. Steelgrade, fireproof

Giant: 1 potion of giants strength. Sets strength to max.

Cyclops: 1 potion of truesight. See through walls.

Ettin: 2 souls instead of one.

Minotaur: 2 bloodsteel battle axes

Hydra: 14 hydra teeth spikes. Irongrade.

Balrog: Volcanic Scimitar and Volcanic Whip. Bad. Ass.

Manticore: 25 manticore bolts. If stuck in enemy, releases toxic gas.

Guilds are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their guild twice as fast. There are 12 guilds in total, you can find more info on them in the "general changes" section.

Joining a guild costs 500 gold. The dwarf will become a guild apprentice and stay inside the workshop for one week, before transforming into a full guild member. Dwarves can also switch guilds or leave them.

The garrison is the military counterpart to the guilds. Military groups are dwarven castes with higher learn rates then normal dwarves. They learn all skills related to their group twice as fast. There are 4 groups in total, you can find more info on them in the "general changes" section.

Joining a group costs 2500 gold and 1 week of time. The dwarf will become a recruit in that time and stay inside the garrison, then transform into a military dwarf. Dwarves can also switch groups or leave them.

The four groups are very distinctive, offensive (Legion), defensive (Guard), ranged (Marksman) and speed (Wrestlers) They are also the only dwarves that can enter martial trances and that get positive thoughts from fighting.

Important: Guilds, Military Groups, Mages and Priests are mutually exclusive. You can't have a masons-guild legionnaire Airmage Apostle.

You can also sound the alarm: It can wake sleeping dwarves and cancels all breakes and parties. This allows your army to be ready for an attack. The reaction is free and can be run as often as you want, but mind that the loud noise and lack of sleep will cause bad thoughts.

You can imprison dwarves here, choosing between 1 to 12 months of incarceration. The dwarf will stay inside the prison as a immobile, mute and blind "imprisoned dwarf". Since they wear shackles you could even move them by using pastures. After the desired amount of time, the dwarf will transform into a normal member of society again.

The reactions are not free. You need drinks and food to sustain the prisoner, one barrel of drink for every 3 months, and one unit of meat for every 1 month. A 10 month incarceration for example would require 10 meat and 4 barrels of drink.

Dwarves will get a permanent malus on strength, toughness and agility. They lose 2% of these attributes for each month they were in prison.

The colosseum allows you to fight creatures. You need a changeling pet for it to work. The changeling is transformed into the monster you want to fight. To create metal colossi as enemies you will also need the "book of summonings" which can be bought from the drow.
