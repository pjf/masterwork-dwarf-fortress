This mod includes 12 Embark profiles. Your skills, pets and items are pre selected for you. Most player prefer their own selection and play style, but I hope that the different profiles help role playing a bit. All of them are either RPG-Embarks or Challenge-Embarks. I also included one for new players that should help them starting a safe fortress.

I also added a last embark profile, which is called empty template. You have neither pets, skills nor items in it, since the "plan for you journey carefully" auto selects many items for you that you have to delete.This profile safes you some time.

At the very bottom you will find profiles marked (ORC) and (KOBOLD) These are for Kobold Camp and Orc Fortress, and should not be used when you play dwarves.

For new Player - Easy Start Build

This setup allows quick mining with 3 miners. Your food will be produced by 2 growers that can use your seeds, as well as 4 birds and nest boxes. The Mechanic can secure your entry quickly, and the bonecarver churns out crafts for the first caravan. The one Diagnoser can safe lives early on. All on all a good setup for people who want a quick fortress that produces its own food from the start on.

Farmers & Shepherds

You lead a group of willing settlers to a new beginning. You brought everything you need to sustain your people. Build a settlement, encourage migrants and start a new life.

With this set-up you will be self-sufficiant from the very beginning. You can wall yourself in and produce everything you need to survive. You have plenty of life stock, some food and drinks and enough seeds to start farming. With 2 Growers, a butcher, a cook and a brewer, you will never lack food or booze. Everything else will go slowly though, with only one miner to dig out the fortress.

Miner Expedition

You are leading an expedition deep into foreign land. You shall make plans for a grand endeavor of dwarvenkind. A team of miners has been selected to go ahead and strike the earth, digging the outlines of the new fortress.

A force of 6 miners and 1 woodcutter. You can easily survive a full year with the booze and food you have. Best used to dig a grand fortress or to start a megaproject, to hollow out the mountain before your real embark crew arrives. Usually works well, at least till the first ambush shows up, after that you can reclaim with your real embark group.

Engineer Force

The dwarven king has sent you forth with a group of well trained and tested engineers. You are tasked with building a solid, unconquerable fortress.

You have 2 miners at your disposal and you can immediatly start buildings traps and siege equipment. The highest skill of your siege-workers is the ambusher skill, giving them a free ranged weapon and ammo at the start. With these archers, the traps and possible ballistas and catapults your fortress should be secure against forces from outside. Designed for players that rely on walls as defense.

Military Expedition

Your expedition force is on the march. A heavily armed group is sent out to pacify even the most haunted corners of the dwarven kingdom.

You have 6 armed dwarves in total and 4 dogs. A doctor is on standby to treat early wounds. The 2 Ambushers get free ranged weapons and ammo at the start. Remember to disable hunting for these dwarves, as they would waste their ammo. The food and booze should last till the first caravan arrives. This setup is for player who like to rely on solid warfare as a defense, those who dislike traps and walls.

Crafter's Guild

A traveling group of craftsmen run out of booze. Without alcohol no one wants to travel any further, so here you are, and here you stay. Luckily you happen to have picks and axes with you, and can build a shelter. Wells should be constructed early, even if that means that you have to drink water to survive. Real water, with 0% alcohol content...

The assortment of different crafting skills should allow high-value trade goods in no time, and you eagerly await the dwarven caravan.

Noble Escort

A challenging embark. You have one worker and 6 useless nobles that refuse physical labor.

You must have angered someone high up the food chain. Probably some useless noble, maybe it is about the monarchs daughter, who knows... Point is, your are in trouble. You have been tasked to escort the bright shining upper class to their new homes. Absolutely untrained in manual labor, these dwarves will be of little help. It is up to you to dig the rooms, fell the trees, build the furniture, grow food and brew drinks. All on your own.

Itemless Embark

All is lost. The fortress is destroyed and the survivors are scattered in the wind. A small group of scavengers have fount together and tries to survive in the wild.

You have no pick, no anvil, no axe... A true challenge. vagabonds that live of the land, staying on the surface... I would advise to disassemble the wagon first, build a carpenter, make 2 wooden training axes, and start chopping wood. Then build a barricade to stay alive. Gather plants for food, and use the wood for a Still and Barrels. Armok willing a caravans might find you here in the wilderness.
