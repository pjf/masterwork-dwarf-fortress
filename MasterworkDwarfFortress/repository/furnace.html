The vanilla kiln has been splitted into the kiln and the clay oven. The clay oven can make any items from clay, fireclay or kaolinite, as well as a unit of fuel. Clay produces stoneware items, Fireclay makes earthenware items and kaolinite produces porcelain items.

The "collect clay" zones have been renamed to "collect clay/dirt/peat", since more types of soil are valid targets now. Dirt can only be used as free boulders for constructions, while peat can be compressed and burned as low-cost fuel.

Important: All ore should be sorted in the Ore Processor before the Smelter can make bars from it.

You can use plywood for one unit of charcoal per log, burn compact peat to 2-4 bars of coke and the very valuable anthracite for 12 bars of coke. Plywood is a byproduct of the wood-industry, compact peat can either be found in soil-layers or produced by compressing peat, and anthrazite is a rare coal cluster found deep underground.

You can create a metal anvil from five bars. You no longer need to bring one at embark.

Smelting ore also creates a waste product called Slag. These bars clutter up your smelter and should be destroyed in the slagpit or used up as buildmats.

Candelabras, Braziers and Fireplaces are made in the craftsman and forge. Torchbearer Statues are a statue and a torch from the Chandlers Workshop. Candelabras need additional candles, while Gems of Light require one large gem. They are in the category of "illumination" and are made in the same workshops that toys have been made before.

If you play with the Rendermax Plugin, these buildings are your light-sources.

They have three reactions. One allows a dwarf to smoke a pipe. You need a smoking pipe and tobacco for this, and it will erase all bad thoughts from the dwarf. The last two can start fires: Large and small fires. You need 1 log for the small one, and 5 logs and 1 fuel for the large fire, which will burn for 9 months.

It has plenty of uses however, from repelling invasion by causing a bushfire, cleaning areas, using a dump-zone to pile refuse on a large fire, or even building one down in the caverns, to get rid of critters and vermin. Remember, even moss or the crystal cavern can burn.

The Ore Processor is very important. All mined mineral bearing rock has to be sorted here, into rock, ore and base-minerals.

Its very simple. You mine "gold bearing rock". You "Process gold bearing rock" and you get pure gold ore and some boulders or blocks. The gold ore can now be used in the smelter. You could even smelt the unprocessed ore, but it would result in little outcome and lots of slag.

The blast furnace has one job and one job only, to make metal bars. You need a big selection of materials to build it, including leather for bellows. It will greatly shorten the amount of time needed for basic metal generation. It cannot handle the more advanced new metals.

As with all magma furnaces, you can fill magmabottles here. These are used in the spawn location to create liquid magma, for moats or traps.

As with all metalworking it produces slag, a waste product that needs to be cleaned up with the slagpit. If you disable the slagpit, no slag should appear ingame. Slag is not stocckpiled. The blast furnace is very slag-efficient, producing 3 times less of it then the smelter.

The crucible is a big melting pot to extract base minerals or purify metals. You can remove the rust from iron and steel, or melt blocks into obsidian and slade. Four blocks give your one obsidian, eight blocks for slade.

The metals are chrome, cobalt, mithril, titanium and wolfram. Chrome is decorational only, cobalt around iron-grade, mithril around steel-grade but very light, titanium decorational only, and wolfram very heavy. All of these have an alloys associated with them in the Metallurgist as well. Most of these minerals can only be found in small clusters, the exception is cobaltite veins.

The most important reaction is the more efficient making of both bronze and steel the crucible allows. You will get one additional bar, as well as a small chance for another one. It is about 65% more efficient then the smelter reactions.

As with all magma furnaces, you can fill magmabottles here. These are used in the spawn location to create liquid magma, for moats or traps.

As with all metalworking it produces slag, a waste product that needs to be cleaned up with the slagpit. If you disable the slagpit, no slag should appear ingame. Slag is not stocckpiled.

You can forge two guns, muskets and flint-lock pistols. Muskets are two-hand weapons that use bullets to do a lot of damage, while pistols are single-hand weapons that are a bit weaker. You can carry two pistols, or a pistol and a shield.

The ammunition is created from several types of metal, in batches of 100 bullets. You need black powder for this, which is created in the Chemist. The high amount gives rifledwarves quite an edge, because they can fire 4 times longer then crossbowdwarves before they must gather new ammo.

You can also add daggers to muskets or pistols, to obtain bayoneted versions that can do more damage in melee combat.

The heavy siegeworks allow the creation of ballista ammo directly from metal. No need to make a wooden bolt and attach a head. You can take bars and smelt 3 ballista bolts in one go.

You can also create catapult and ballista parts from metals.

The most interesting addition is the improvement you can do to existing ammo. You can create plate armor in this workshop. It is the best armor in the game when it comes to coverage. You can also create sets of platearmor. The set costs exactly the same as making the items one by one, but is more fuel efficient.

Warbeast Kennels -

Build labor

building_war_kennels.txt
reaction_war_kennels.txt

You can train animals in this workshop. First you need to bring the animal to the kennels. Simply make a pasture on top of the workshop and assign the wanted animals there. You can do as many as you want in one go. Then run the reactions.

Combat training costs 1 stack of bones and trains all nearby creatures of the selected type. They will get 1 additional level in biting, dodging and melee combat, up till legendary.

You can armor a small list of animals with either iron or steel armor here. Shaggy Badgerdogs and Cragtooth Boars, Mastiffs, Horses, Grizzlies, Raptors, Elephants, Unicorns and more. This will both increase their defensive as well as offensive capabilities. Quite a lot, actually. You need iron or steel bars and mechanisms for this. If an armored creature is butchered, it will also create an inedible metal part. These parts can be recycled to gain back a bit of the metal.

Rune-Engraving an armored animal will triple its attributes.

An Example: The result will be a legendary biter, dodger and fighter steelclad war-mastiffs with three times the strength, toughness and agility as a normal one.

WARNING: Armored animals do not breed. Due to game mechanics they would give birth to already armored children. Don't armor all your warbeasts if you still want to breed them.

Weaponsmith

5x5
Researcher, Magma
building_golemforge.txt
reaction_golemforge.txt

Create powerful metal warriors. You need to sacrifice one dwarf though, who will be encased in the metal golem. You can choose between 7 different metals and 3 types of golems. You can also do 3 kinds of upgrades. Make golems stronger, faster or give them an AoE attack. You don't get an announcement for those, but the golem will blink for the first 1,5 months upon receiving an upgrade. You will need clockwork parts for these, which are gained from Automatons, or by researching them in the Researchers Study.

To create a golem you need 5 bars, 2 mechanisms and one weapon. You need use a spear, a warhammer or a long sword. The dwarf running the reaction might transform into a golem then.

Golems, once completed, cant be assigned roles, so make sure that you only transform members of the military. They will stay in the squad and will follow order. Golems will do no labor other then war. Although, I once had a golem mayor. He was awesome.

Last step is upgrading and recycling golems. Upgrading required mechanisms and adds either strength, speed, an AoE Interaction, or skills. When you train skills it will add 1 level per upgrade, so you can upgrade each golem 15 times till you have a legendary fighter. Dead golems leave a corpse that can be processed back into metal bars and mechanisms.

Evil mages can be created in this altar.You can only have 1 mage of each type at the same time.

building_warpstone_pool.txt
reaction_warpstone_pool.txt

Can transform any pet into a changeling or sprite using a single warpstone boulder. This is important because many other workshops and reactions use Changelings and Sprites as base-creatures, to create something new.

Magmalings and Waterlings and more require these base-creatures, as do Familiars.

You can also create tame Forgotten Beasts in this workshop. They are randomly created and you will get different ones in every world you generate.

(Future additions are planned)

A loo, for people that like to give their dwarves any luxury they can think of. Plumbing is optional.

This building is decorational and has only one set of reactions, to empty out buckets, barrels and large pots. You can designate what kind of content you want to dump, from about a dozen types.
